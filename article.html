The Shadow War Out of the Shadows: Iran and Israel Conflict

Published by Paudel & Team • Current Issues

For decades, the conflict between Iran and Israel was fought in the shadows. It was a war of proxies, cyberattacks, and covert operations. However, recent escalations have brought this rivalry into a direct, open confrontation, reshaping the entire geopolitical landscape of the Middle East.

The root of the animosity lies deep within ideological shifts following the 1979 Islamic Revolution in Iran. Since then, Iran has established a "Axis of Resistance," supporting various militant groups across the region. Israel views Iran’s rising influence and its advancing nuclear program as an existential threat.

With both nations exchanging direct missile strikes, the fear of a full-scale regional war has never been higher. The international community remains on high alert, as any further escalation could disrupt global oil supplies and trigger unpredictable economic chain reactions worldwide. As diplomacy struggles to find a footing, the world watches closely to see if a peaceful resolution is still possible.
